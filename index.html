the problem

The wrong people get
through. The right ones
get filtered out.

•
Screening filters for vocabulary, not capability.

Applicant tracking systems match keywords because no one has defined what good actually looks like. The candidate who did the work gets filtered out for describing it differently. The candidate who learned the right words gets through.

•
Without defined criteria, there is no consistent standard.

The evaluation criteria are reconstructed after the fact to match whoever impressed. Every hire starts from scratch: the same gaps that produced the wrong outcome last time are carried forward unchanged.

•
Candidates learn nothing.

Applications disappear. Rejections explain nothing about what was evaluated, how close they came, or what it would take to succeed. The rational response is volume: apply to everything, tailor for nothing.

the solution

Criteria first.
Everything else follows.

→
The hiring manager defines what the role requires before any candidate enters.

The criteria are specific, locked, and applied consistently to every candidate: the scoring surface never changes mid-pool.

→
Every candidate has a thirty-minute conversation about their actual work.

What they have done is measured semantically against what the role requires; not whether they used the right words to describe it.

→
The top candidates advance automatically.

No manual shortlisting. The criteria the manager defined make the advancement decision.

→
Every candidate who completes the assessment receives comparative feedback.

Where they stood against the pool on each dimension; not silence and a template rejection.
