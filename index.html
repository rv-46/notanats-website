the problem

Screening breaks down before
the first application lands.

•
Keyword matching does not measure capability.

Applicant tracking systems scan resumes for vocabulary because no one has defined what good actually looks like. The candidate who did the work gets filtered out for describing it differently. The candidate who knows the right words gets through without having done it.

•
Criteria shift after the first impressive candidate arrives.

Without locked dimensions, evaluation standards reconstruct themselves to match whoever stood out. Every pool measures something different. The same gaps that produced the wrong hire last time carry forward into the next search.

•
Candidates stop hearing back, so they apply everywhere poorly.

Applications disappear. Rejections explain nothing about what was evaluated or where the candidate fell short. The rational response is volume over quality. Hiring teams get buried in applications that do not fit because applicants cannot tell the difference.

•
Screening hours produce no reusable intelligence.

Every search restarts from scratch. The work of reviewing dozens of applications yields no standard, no comparison, and no feedback for future searches. The time spent reading resumes is lost when the next role opens.

the solution

Criteria first.
Everything else follows.

→
Criteria-first calibration before any candidate applies.

The hiring manager defines what the role requires. The system challenges vague dimensions until each one is specific enough to score against. The scoring surface is locked before a single candidate sees the link.

→
Conversation-based assessment instead of resume screening.

Every candidate has a 30-minute conversation about their actual work. Semantic scoring measures what they have done against what the role requires. There is no rubric to reverse-engineer.

→
The scoring rubric is hidden from candidates.

Candidates cannot see what is being evaluated. Performing well requires having actually done the work. There is no keyword list to optimize for and no rubric to game.

→
Feedback for every candidate, not just the ones who advanced.

When the process closes, every candidate who completed the assessment receives feedback. Where they stood against the pool on each dimension. A real outcome instead of silence and a template rejection.

what makes it different

Seven things no other
screening tool does.

Not differentiators on a slide deck. Differentiators in the product.

01

Criteria-first, not filter-first.

The manager defines what the role requires before any candidate enters. Every candidate is evaluated against the same locked definition; not against whoever applied last week.

02

Conversation, not resume.

Every candidate has a 30-minute conversation about their actual work. Semantic scoring measures what they have done against what the role requires; not whether they used the right words.

03

The scoring surface is hidden.

Candidates cannot see what is being evaluated. Performing well requires having actually done the work. There is no rubric to optimize for.

04

The pool improves after every interview.

After interviews, the manager records what the screening got right and wrong. The criteria are refined. The entire candidate pool is rescored. Candidates who did not advance the first time may surface under the updated model.

05

Every candidate gets a result.

When the process closes, every candidate who completed the assessment receives feedback: where they stood against the pool on each dimension. Not a template rejection. A real outcome.

06

Gets more accurate with every hire.

A 3-month pulse check after each hire feeds back into the calibration model. The screening for the next hire in this role starts from evidence, not estimates.
